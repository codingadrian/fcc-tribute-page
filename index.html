Pabru Presbere

The most feared warrior in Talamanca

Here's a timeline of Pabru Presbere's life and some interesting facts:

  • 1670 ~ Birthday is unknown, location is thought to be Suinse, Talamanca.
  • Pabru ~ is a Bribri word meaning "chief of the macaws".
  • Usekar ~ he is thought to have been a major religous figure and to posses supernatural powers
  • 1706 ~ Pabru is baptised as "Pablo" by Fray Pablo de Rebudilla and Antonio de Zamora.
  • The Letter ~ Pabru intercepts a letter saying ht e indians will be removed from their land and forced to move to other villages.
  • The Reunion ~ Pabru convices other caciques to rise against the Spaniards, they begin to make spears and shields.
  • Urinama ~ Pabru attacks Urinama on September 28th of 1709. The mob kills all the Frays and soldiers, along with a woman and a child.
  • The Fires ~ The rebels burnt 14 churches, the monasteries and the cabildo houses.
  • The Capture ~ The Spaniards brought 200 men to Talamanca, 700 indians were captured, among them Pabru.
  • The Road of Death ~ Sarampion and mistreatment killed most of those 700 indians.
  • The Sentence ~ Pabru was sentenced to death by the use of the "Garrote Vil".
  • The Execution ~ The "Garrote Vil" could not be administered; so he was shot with an arcabuz and then beheaded.
  • The Legacy ~ After the 1710 rebellion, the Spanish abandonded their ambitions in Talamanca, it remained free and a heaven for other indians escaping from the Conquest.
  • Memory ~ Pabru is remembered as the most feared warrior in talamanca and Talamanca nowadays belongs to the indians thanks to Pabru and his brothers and sisters.

Pabru was the keeper of traditions and the keeper of the land. He joined fighting clans and fought along their side to make Talamanca free.
